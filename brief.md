## References used

### 1. groomit.me (competitor reference)
- Hero composition: Benefit-first value prop with 4.8-star rating (45,680 reviews) as immediate social proof
- Section order: Ratings > Why Choose Us > Groomer Profiles > Testimonials > FAQs > Booking (reassurance before transaction)
- Copy voice: Conversational directness, short declarative sentences. "No calls, No waiting." Modern SaaS tone adapted for pet services
- Photo treatment: Individual groomer profiles with names, experience levels, ratings. Verified customer reviews mention specific pet names
- Trust signals: "180K+ Pets Groomed", "50+ U.S. cities", individual groomer years of experience, background checks, platform-level insurance
- Conversion path: Book easily through platform, see real-time availability
- **Apply to this build:** (1) Lead with specific review count (54) and rating (4.7) as immediate hero trust badge, (2) reassurance-before-transaction section order, (3) name specific groomer experience/qualifications, (4) use oddly-specific stats not round numbers

### 2. dwell.com (non-competitor, editorial/magazine)
- Hero composition: Featured article + high-resolution image, editorial authority before navigation
- Section order: Editorial-first sequencing, inspiration precedes commerce
- Copy voice: Balances specificity with accessibility. Curiosity-driven phrasing with substance. Avoids jargon and oversimplification
- Photo treatment: Consistent attention to natural light, material texture, and spatial relationships
- Trust signals: Named architects, specific locations, price transparency, bylines. "Editorial Standards" footer link
- Conversion path: Free editorial > premium subscription as natural progression
- **Apply to this build:** (1) Editorial-quality photo treatment with warm natural lighting, (2) specificity in copy (name the breeds, name the products, name the techniques), (3) content hierarchy that builds trust before asking for the call, (4) clean typography with clear hierarchy, generous whitespace

### Cross-reference design thesis
Lead with a warm, editorially-clean hero that pairs a real grooming photo with the specific trust number (4.7 stars, 54 reviews). Structure follows reassurance-before-transaction: show the work, name the services with specificity (breed-appropriate cuts, not "full groom"), then ask for the call. Photo treatment should be warm and natural, not sterile studio shots. Keep copy direct and short-sentenced like groomit.me but with the editorial specificity of dwell.com.
